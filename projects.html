Project One

Designed for researchers and policymakers in public health and global health. This interactive tool (accompanied by a detailed PDF report) leverages the latest global mortality data to enable country-by-country comparisons, age- and sex-specific breakdowns, and decadal trend analysis. Users can explore patterns in total deaths and death rates across time (1970 to 2010) and among different population cohorts.

Project Analysis
Country and global comparisons
Filter and compare mortality metrics across countries and regions.
For example, the dashboard shows that India and China account for the highest number of deaths
(188M and 157M respectively, over 1970–2010), while also allowing direct comparison of age-adjusted death rates.
Age- and sex-specific insights
Examine how mortality burden is distributed by age group and by sex.
Older cohorts bear the largest share of deaths (e.g. ~75 million among those 80+ years),
reflecting demographic aging. Sex filters reveal roughly balanced mortality shares between males and females,
enabling analysis of disparities and trends.
Decadal trends
Analyze changes over time in total deaths and average death rates.
The tool highlights that global average death rates have declined substantially
(from ~5854 to 4753 per 100k population) in recent decades, even as absolute death counts continue to rise.
This key insight (from the analysis)shows that “despite global declines in average death rates, total deaths continue to rise, driven largely by population growth and aging”.
Evidence-based policy support
By combining comprehensive data with visual analytics, the dashboard provides an evidence-based foundation for health policy.
For example, identifying countries or age groups with slower mortality declines can help target interventions.

Project Three

Power Distribution Insights: Consumption & Weather Dynamics, Morocco 2017
Data Source: Maven Analytics
Tool used: Power bI
Data Cleaning: I used conditional formatting to set a condition for the weather columns.
Dax was extensively used to calculate all anchor measurements.
📉 Key Insights
⚡Power Consumption and Weather Patterns🌤
Power consumption peaks during the warmer months, especially in July and August, driven by higher temperatures and increased humidity.
The average annual temperature is 18.81°C, with significant increases in power demand for cooling systems during hot months. Humidity and windspeed also influence energy consumption, with the highest usage observed when conditions are humid and winds are calm.
🏤Zonal Analysis
Zone 1 has the highest energy consumption at 1.7 billion KWH, with notable peaks during the summer due to diffuse solar radiation.
Zone 2 shows a more balanced consumption pattern, with a total of 1.10 billion KWH, reflecting moderate peaks in summer.
Zone 3 consumes 934.86 million KWH, with lower overall usage but still demonstrating seasonal peaks during warmer months.
📊 Effect of Diffuse Flows
Diffuse solar radiation impacts power consumption, especially in Zones 1 and 2, aligning with the highest demand periods.
Understanding this relationship offers opportunities to optimize energy distribution, potentially leveraging solar power during peak times. Recommendation
The following recommendations are proposed to optimize energy distribution and manage seasonal demand effectively:
1. Leverage Renewable Energy Sources.
2. Enhance Energy Efficiency in high-demand Zones.
3. Improve Grid Reliability for Seasonal peak.
4. Predictive Weather and Consumption Monitoring.
